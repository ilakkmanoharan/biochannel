# 🎤 **3-Minute Demo Day Script (Synced to Slides)**

---

## ⏱️ **Slide 1 — Title (0:00–0:15)**

**Decision Biology — modeling how cells make decisions under noise.**

Biology today can observe what cells do…
but it struggles to explain *why* outcomes vary so much.

**[Pause – 1 sec]**

We’re building a system that turns cellular behavior into something measurable and predictable.

---

## ⏱️ **Slide 2 — Problem (0:15–0:35)**

Cells make decisions under noise—
but we don’t have a way to measure how reliable those decisions are.

The same signal can produce completely different outcomes…
even in identical cells.

**[Pause – 0.5 sec]**

We can measure gene expression…
but not how faithfully signals are processed.

---

## ⏱️ **Slide 3 — Why It Matters (0:35–0:55)**

This lack of reliability shows up everywhere:

* Drugs work in some cells… and fail in others
* Diseases behave unpredictably
* Biological models often don’t generalize

At its core, this is a **signal reliability problem**.

---

## ⏱️ **Slide 4 — Insight (0:55–1:15)**

Our key insight is simple:

Cells behave like communication systems.

Signals go in…
biological pathways process them…
and gene expression comes out.

**[Pause – 0.5 sec]**

But noise limits how much information is preserved.

So instead of just observing biology—
we measure how much signal survives.

---

## ⏱️ **Slide 5 — Solution (1:15–1:35)**

We built a system that quantifies cellular decision-making.

It models cells as noisy information channels,
measures information flow from input to output,
and simulates how decisions change under different conditions.

This gives us a **quantitative view of biological reliability**.

---

## ⏱️ **Slide 6 — Product Demo (1:35–2:00)**

We use real biological datasets:

Single-cell RNA-seq shows cell decisions as clusters—
each cluster represents a distinct state.

Time-series data lets us measure information loss—
how much of the original signal is preserved.

Drug response data predicts sensitivity and resistance—
how cells respond to interventions.

---

## ⏱️ **Slide 7 — What You Can Do (2:00–2:20)**

With this system, users can:

* Measure how reliable signaling pathways are
* Visualize variability across cells
* Predict how interventions change outcomes

**[Pause – 0.5 sec]**

This connects data, dynamics, and decision-making in one framework.

---

## ⏱️ **Slide 8 — Why Now (2:20–2:35)**

This is possible now because:

* We have massive single-cell datasets
* Compute and AI models have matured
* But there’s still no unified framework

We’re filling that gap.

---

## ⏱️ **Slide 9 — Vision (2:35–2:50)**

Our vision is to move biology:

From observation…
to prediction…
to design.

Where we can not only understand decisions—
but engineer them.

---

## ⏱️ **Slide 10 — Closing (2:50–3:00)**

We’re building the first system that quantifies
how cells make decisions under noise.

**[Pause – 1 sec]**

And once you can measure it—
you can predict it…
and ultimately, design it.
