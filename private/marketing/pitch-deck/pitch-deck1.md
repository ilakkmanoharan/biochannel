# 🚀 **Demo Day Pitch Deck (Decision Biology)**

---

## **Slide 1 — Title**

**Decision Biology**
*Modeling how cells make decisions under noise*

**Subtitle (small):**
Turning biology into a measurable, predictive system

### 🎤 Speaker Notes

We’re building Decision Biology—an information-theoretic system to understand how cells process signals and make decisions under uncertainty.

---

## **Slide 2 — Problem**

Cells make decisions under noise—
but we can’t measure how reliable those decisions are.

### Supporting bullets:

* Same signal → different outcomes
* High variability across cells
* No metric for signal fidelity

### 🎤 Speaker Notes

Biology is fundamentally noisy. Even identical cells respond differently to the same signal. Today, we can observe this—but we cannot quantify how reliable those decisions are.

---

## **Slide 3 — Why It Matters**

Unreliable cellular decisions drive:

* Drug resistance
* Disease variability
* Failed predictions in biology

### 🎤 Speaker Notes

This lack of reliability measurement limits our ability to predict outcomes—especially in areas like drug response and disease progression.

---

## **Slide 4 — Insight**

Cells behave like communication systems.

```text
Signal → Pathway → Gene Expression
```

Noise limits how much information is preserved.

### 🎤 Speaker Notes

We model cells as information-processing systems. Signals go through noisy pathways, and gene expression is the output. This lets us apply information theory to biology.

---

## **Slide 5 — Solution**

A system that quantifies cellular decision-making.

* Models cells as noisy channels
* Measures information flow
* Simulates outcomes under perturbations

### 🎤 Speaker Notes

We built a system that brings together biological data and information theory to quantify how cells process signals and make decisions.

---

## **Slide 6 — Product Demo**

Using real datasets:

* Single-cell RNA-seq → cell decisions as clusters
* Time-series → information loss
* Drug response → sensitivity & resistance

### 🎤 Speaker Notes

We use real biological datasets. Single-cell data shows decision states, time-series data measures signal fidelity, and drug datasets model interventions.

---

## **Slide 7 — What You Can Do**

* Measure signal reliability
* Visualize variability across cells
* Predict intervention outcomes

### 🎤 Speaker Notes

This system enables users to quantify how reliable signaling is, understand variability, and predict how interventions change outcomes.

---

## **Slide 8 — Why Now**

* Explosion of single-cell data
* Advances in AI + modeling
* No unified framework yet

### 🎤 Speaker Notes

We now have the data and compute—but no unified framework to interpret it through the lens of information flow.

---

## **Slide 9 — Vision**

From observation → prediction → design

Toward programmable biological systems

### 🎤 Speaker Notes

Our long-term vision is to move biology from descriptive science to engineered systems—where we can predict and design outcomes.

---

## **Slide 10 — Closing**

We’re building the first system that quantifies
how cells make decisions under noise.

### 🎤 Speaker Notes

This is the missing layer in biology—measuring reliability. Once we have that, prediction and design become possible.
