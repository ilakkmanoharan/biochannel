## 🧬 What does “cells make decisions” mean?

Cells are constantly choosing between outcomes, for example:

* Divide vs stop growing
* Repair damage vs trigger cell death
* Activate a gene vs keep it off
* Respond to a signal vs ignore it

These are not conscious decisions—but they are **biochemical outcomes** driven by signaling pathways and gene regulation.

---

## 🌫️ What does “under noise” mean?

Inside a cell, everything is inherently noisy because:

* Molecules exist in **small numbers**
* Chemical reactions are **probabilistic**
* Signals fluctuate over time
* Even identical cells behave differently

So the same input signal can produce **different outcomes** in different cells.

That variability is what we call **biological noise**.

---

## 📡 What does “reliability” mean here?

Reliability asks:

> If a cell receives a signal… how consistently does it produce the correct response?

In other words:

* Does the signal get transmitted clearly?
* Or does noise distort it?

This is similar to communication systems:

```text
Input signal → noisy channel → output
```

If the output closely matches the input → **high reliability**
If it’s distorted or inconsistent → **low reliability**

---

## ❗ What’s missing today?

We can:

* Measure gene expression
* Observe cell behavior
* Identify pathways

But we **don’t have a standard way to quantify**:

* How much information a cell preserves from input → output
* How much is lost due to noise
* How reliable a signaling pathway really is

We lack a **metric of fidelity**.

---

## 🧠 Why this is important

Without measuring reliability:

* We don’t know why cells behave inconsistently
* We can’t predict outcomes accurately
* Drug responses remain variable and uncertain

But if we could measure it:

* We could compare pathways quantitatively
* Identify failure points in signaling
* Design better interventions

Cells receive signals, but because of noise, their responses vary—and today we don’t have a way to measure how faithfully those signals are translated into outcomes.
