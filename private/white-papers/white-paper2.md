White Paper 2: Bulk RNA Sequencing (RNA-seq)
Population-Level Gene Expression and Regulatory Signals
Abstract
Bulk RNA sequencing measures gene expression averaged across a population of cells. While lacking single-cell resolution, it provides robust signals for detecting differential expression and global regulatory patterns.
1. Biological Basis
In tissues, gene expression reflects:
Aggregate activity of many cells
Dominant cell types and processes
Bulk measurements capture:
Strong transcriptional signals
System-wide responses (e.g., disease vs healthy)
2. Measurement Principles
RNA is extracted from a sample and sequenced.
Output:
Gene expression vector per sample
3. Statistical Structure
Reduced sparsity compared to scRNA-seq
Higher signal-to-noise ratio
Loss of cell-level heterogeneity
4. Scientific Interpretation
Bulk RNA-seq reveals:
Differential gene expression between conditions
Pathway activation
Disease signatures
However, it cannot:
Distinguish cell subpopulations
Capture rare cell states
5. Scientific Role
Bulk RNA-seq serves as:
A baseline for population-level biology
A validation layer for single-cell findings
