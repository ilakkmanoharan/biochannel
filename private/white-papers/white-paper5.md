White Paper 5: Signaling Pathways and Gene Regulatory Networks
Information Processing in Cellular Systems
Abstract
Cells sense and respond to their environment through signaling pathways and gene regulatory networks. These systems transform external signals into gene expression outputs, often under conditions of noise and uncertainty.
1. Biological Basis
Key components:
Receptors
Signaling cascades
Transcription factors
These form networks that:
Amplify signals
Filter noise
Generate decisions
2. Network Structure
Biological networks exhibit:
Feedback loops
Redundancy
Nonlinearity
3. Information Transmission
Cells process signals:
With limited fidelity
Under stochastic fluctuations
Key constraints:
Noise
Limited molecular counts
Energy constraints
4. Data Representation
Datasets include:
Gene expression profiles
Protein interactions
Pathway maps
5. Scientific Insights
These systems explain:
How cells make decisions
Why responses vary across cells
Limits of biological information processing
