White Paper 3: Single-Cell RNA-seq in Cancer Biology
Tumor Heterogeneity and Evolutionary Dynamics
Abstract
Cancer is not a uniform disease but a heterogeneous ecosystem of cells. Single-cell RNA sequencing enables the characterization of tumor subpopulations, revealing evolutionary dynamics and therapeutic resistance mechanisms.
1. Biological Basis
Tumors consist of:
Malignant cells
Immune cells
Stromal cells
Each subpopulation exhibits distinct gene expression patterns.
2. Evolutionary Dynamics
Cancer progression involves:
Mutation
Selection
Clonal expansion
This creates:
Intratumoral heterogeneity
Diverse cellular phenotypes
3. Data Characteristics
scRNA-seq captures:
Rare resistant cell populations
Transitional states
Immune interactions
4. Scientific Insights
Reveals:
Drug resistance pathways
Tumor microenvironment interactions
Lineage relationships between cancer cells
5. Implications
Understanding heterogeneity is critical for:
Precision medicine
Targeted therapies
Predicting treatment outcomes
